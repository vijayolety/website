✨ My Story

Building things that matter.

I've spent 19 years, turning "what if" into "what is."

The Scars and the Successes

I don't just build software; I build the teams that make the impossible feel normal. My journey started with a simple thought: how do you take a raw, messy idea and turn it into something real?

I found the answer in the scrappy, late-night chaos of startups. Being a Founding Member at ventures like Multipl and 47Line wasn't just a career move—it was a mission. From that first line of code at 47Line to the day we were acquired by Cisco, it’s been about more than just scale. It’s been about the people I grew with and the products we truly sweated over.

What I Live By

  • People over Pixels: Tools change every year, but human drive is the only constant. I build teams that own their work, not just their JIRA tickets.
  • The Human Why: If it’s not solving a real problem for a real person, it’s just noise. I bridge the gap between business dreams and technical reality.
  • The 0-to-1 Pulse: There’s a specific kind of magic in the early days. I know how to keep that fire alive even as we scale to millions of users.
Perspective

The Big Picture

I grew up as an engineer in rooms where small mistakes had long shadows. At LG, I learnt what it means to ship for the real world, where software leaves your laptop and lands in people's hands. At Cisco, I saw how discipline, trust, and scale are built quietly, one good decision at a time.

Startups gave me the other half of the story: the urgency, the doubt, the late nights, the joy of watching a rough idea become something people actually depend on. That mix shaped me. I care about velocity, but not at the cost of trust. I care about scale, but not if the team breaks on the way there.
